# MIT AI Governance Mapping

## What this skill does
Operator-grade governance-mapping support for comparing laws, standards, frameworks, and guidance through a single coverage lens.

## Who it is for
- Teams comparing governance documents instead of working inside a single framework.
- Operators identifying where coverage is weak, fragmented, or redundant.
- Reviewers who need a portfolio-level map of governance material across sectors or jurisdictions.

## Use this skill when
- You need to compare laws, standards, or framework documents side by side.
- You need to find governance gaps, overlaps, or blind spots.
- You need a coverage matrix or executive memo on governance priorities.

## Typical inputs
- Document set, jurisdiction or sector scope, and the coverage lens you care about.
- Known gaps, pain points, lifecycle stages, or actor roles already in debate.
- Decision target such as policy refresh, research note, or executive briefing.

## Typical outputs
- Coverage matrix, governance gap summary, or priority log.
- Comparison memo across laws, standards, or guidance sets.
- Executive summary that routes the next workstream into the right skill.

## How it works
- Normalize the document set before judging what is missing or duplicated.
- Map coverage by risk, actor, lifecycle, sector, or legal status.
- Translate the mapping into a short list of next-step actions and owners.
