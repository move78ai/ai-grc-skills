# MIT AI Risk Repository

## What this skill does
Workflow-first MIT AI Risk Repository guidance for risk classification, prioritization, mitigation planning, and executive summaries.

## Who it is for
- Teams using MIT's public risk taxonomy to structure AI risk analysis.
- Operators turning a broad set of risk signals into a prioritized register.
- Reviewers who need mitigation plans and executive summaries rooted in named risk types.

## Use this skill when
- You need a practical risk classification or prioritization workflow.
- You need a risk register, mitigation plan, or evidence request list.
- You need to route identified risks into governance, supplier, incident, or security follow-up.

## Typical inputs
- System or use case profile, deployment context, and affected stakeholders.
- Observed issues, known controls, incidents, supplier concerns, and open questions.
- Any existing risk scoring, priority thresholds, or governance decisions.

## Typical outputs
- MIT-aligned risk register or mitigation plan.
- Gap analysis, evidence request list, or executive risk summary.
- Decision-ready view of what should be accepted, treated, or escalated.

## How it works
- Use the MIT taxonomy to name and cluster the risks before scoring them.
- Turn prioritized risks into owners, mitigations, and evidence requests.
- Route unresolved risk themes into the narrower skills that can close them.
