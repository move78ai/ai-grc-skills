# Content Provenance / Synthetic Media Transparency

## What this skill does
Workflow-first provenance and transparency guidance for media workflows, synthetic content labels, supplier checks, and rollout planning.

## Who it is for
- Teams deciding when provenance, disclosure, or content credentials should apply.
- Operators designing rollout plans for synthetic-media transparency controls.
- Reviewers assessing whether third-party tools can support the required provenance and labeling posture.

## Use this skill when
- You need a provenance or synthetic-media transparency design for a media workflow.
- You need labeling or disclosure logic for generated or altered content.
- You need supplier capability review or executive rollout planning for content provenance controls.

## Typical inputs
- Media workflow description, generation or editing steps, and distribution channels.
- Existing provenance tooling, suppliers, content types, and user touchpoints.
- Known disclosure obligations, trust-and-safety concerns, and rollout constraints.

## Typical outputs
- Content-credential rollout plan or disclosure decision log.
- Transparency memo, supplier evidence request, or monitoring checklist.
- Executive summary of provenance gaps, constraints, and next actions.

## How it works
- Classify the workflow before deciding whether provenance or labeling is required.
- Separate user-facing notices from backend credential or verification controls.
- End with rollout conditions, owner assignments, and fallback steps where provenance is incomplete.
