# OECD AI Governance and Classification

## What this skill does
Workflow-first OECD AI classification and governance guidance for inventories, lifecycle review, and principle-to-action mapping.

## Who it is for
- Teams classifying AI systems or portfolio slices before deciding governance depth.
- Operators who need to turn OECD principles into action lists and owner assignments.
- Reviewers who need inventory-ready records and governance summaries.

## Use this skill when
- You need to classify an AI system, model, agent, or use case.
- You need lifecycle-stage governance requirements or inventory fields.
- You need a principle-to-action operating plan or executive summary.

## Typical inputs
- System purpose, deployment context, lifecycle stage, and affected stakeholders.
- Known actor roles, suppliers, dependencies, and decision points.
- Current inventory details, governance controls, or unresolved classification questions.

## Typical outputs
- Classification profile and inventory-ready record.
- Lifecycle governance checklist or operating plan.
- Executive summary with evidence requests and next steps.

## How it works
- Classify the object in scope before assigning governance demands.
- Map lifecycle stage and principle-level expectations into concrete actions.
- Use the resulting classification to route into deeper framework or regulatory skills.
