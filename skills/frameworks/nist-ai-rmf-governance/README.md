# NIST AI RMF Governance

## What this skill does
Workflow-first NIST AI RMF guidance for gap analysis, profiles, TEVV planning, GenAI screening, and crosswalk work.

## Who it is for
- Teams adopting NIST AI RMF as an operating model.
- Operators who need usable profiles, gap analyses, and action registers.
- Reviewers comparing NIST AI RMF to ISO/IEC 42001, the EU AI Act, or cyber-AI work.

## Use this skill when
- You need a practical AI RMF gap analysis or governance profile.
- You need to separate baseline AI RMF work from GenAI or cyber-AI work.
- You need crosswalk support into another framework or law.

## Typical inputs
- System context, lifecycle stage, actor roles, and deployment environment.
- Known trustworthiness concerns, incidents, testing evidence, or inventory records.
- Target outcome such as profile drafting, roadmap design, or crosswalk support.

## Typical outputs
- AI RMF gap analysis with prioritized actions.
- System profile, risk register, or TEVV plan.
- Crosswalk memo or cyber-AI prioritization note.

## How it works
- Start with system context and trustworthiness objectives before drafting controls.
- Convert findings into evidence-backed artifacts and owners.
- Treat GenAI and cyber-AI overlays as targeted lenses, not default assumptions.
