# Colorado AI Act Impact Assessment

## What this skill does
Operator-grade Colorado AI Act workflows for high-risk screening, deployer impact assessments, notices, and retained evidence.

## Who it is for
- Teams screening whether a Colorado use case is likely high-risk.
- Operators preparing deployer impact assessments or annual review artifacts.
- Reviewers drafting consumer notices, adverse-decision explanations, or developer evidence requests.

## Use this skill when
- You need to decide whether a system is in scope as a high-risk AI system.
- You need an impact assessment, policy component, or annual review structure.
- You need consumer-facing notices, documentation requests, or incident-related retained records.

## Typical inputs
- Use case description, decision context, deployment setting, and affected consumers.
- Known model providers, developer documentation, and risk-management controls.
- Existing notice language, review cadence, or governance records.

## Typical outputs
- High-risk screening memo or impact assessment.
- Consumer notice pack or adverse-decision explanation draft.
- Policy actions, evidence requests, and review tracker.

## How it works
- Screen for consequential-decision and high-risk triggers first.
- Translate deployer duties into retained artifacts and operating controls.
- Keep risk assessment, consumer communication, and documentation requests clearly separated.
