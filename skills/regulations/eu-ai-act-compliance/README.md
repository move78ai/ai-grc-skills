# EU AI Act Compliance

## What this skill does
Workflow-first EU AI Act support for scope screening, high-risk analysis, FRIA work, GPAI readiness, and evidence planning.

## Who it is for
- Providers, deployers, and mixed-role teams assessing EU AI Act exposure.
- Operators who need screening, FRIA, or evidence planning tied to real articles and annexes.
- Reviewers who need provider and deployer obligations kept separate.

## Use this skill when
- You need to screen whether a use case is prohibited, high-risk, GPAI-related, or likely out of scope.
- You need provider or deployer gap analysis, FRIA support, or technical-documentation planning.
- You need transparency, labeling, post-market monitoring, or GPAI readiness work.

## Typical inputs
- Operator role, intended purpose, market or output destination, and affected persons.
- System architecture, Annex III triggers, GPAI model involvement, and deployment context.
- Existing policies, technical documentation, monitoring, or complaint-handling artifacts.

## Typical outputs
- Risk-classification memo or screening memo.
- High-risk gap analysis, FRIA starter, or provider/deployer action plan.
- Technical-documentation starter, monitoring plan, or transparency notice pack.

## How it works
- Separate scope, prohibited-practice screening, classification, and role-based obligations.
- Tie every obligation to an evidence artifact or operating document.
- Flag where legal certainty depends on missing facts or counsel review.
