# South Korea High-Impact AI Readiness

## What this skill does
Workflow-first South Korea AI Basic Act readiness support for high-impact screening, labeling, operator controls, and evidence packs.

## Who it is for
- Teams screening whether a system may qualify as high-impact AI in South Korea.
- Operators preparing transparency, labeling, or user-protection artifacts.
- Reviewers translating open legal variables into usable readiness documents.

## Use this skill when
- You need high-impact screening or readiness analysis for South Korea.
- You need transparency, GenAI labeling, or synthetic-media notice planning.
- You need operator controls, impact assessment, or domestic-representative readiness support.

## Typical inputs
- System purpose, deployment market, affected users, and public-sector involvement.
- GenAI or synthetic-media usage, explanation expectations, and current controls.
- Known operator role, retained documents, and unresolved legal variables.

## Typical outputs
- High-impact screening memo or readiness gap analysis.
- Transparency and labeling pack or operator-controls pack.
- Impact assessment starter, decision memo, or evidence checklist.

## How it works
- Separate scope, high-impact status, operator duties, and decree-dependent uncertainties.
- Turn the Act into evidence-backed readiness artifacts instead of article-by-article summaries.
- End with the shortest actionable list of documents, owners, and next decisions.
