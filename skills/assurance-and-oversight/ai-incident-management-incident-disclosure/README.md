# AI Incident Management / Incident Disclosure

## What this skill does
Workflow-first incident guidance for AI triage, containment, disclosure decisions, evidence preservation, and after-action work.

## Who it is for
- Teams designing or improving an AI incident-management operating model.
- Operators triaging a live or hypothetical AI incident.
- Reviewers preparing disclosure records, notices, or after-action materials.

## Use this skill when
- You need incident triage, severity logic, or containment planning.
- You need a disclosure decision record or stakeholder notice draft.
- You need a program review, gap assessment, or after-action package.

## Typical inputs
- Incident facts, affected systems or versions, detection source, and user impact.
- Known evidence, third-party involvement, regulatory exposure, and current controls.
- Escalation rules, decision-makers, and any communications already sent.

## Typical outputs
- Incident triage memo or severity assessment.
- Disclosure decision record, notice draft, or board briefing.
- After-action review, remediation tracker, or incident-program gap analysis.

## How it works
- Separate confirmed facts from assumptions, hypotheses, and pending verification.
- Keep severity, containment, disclosure, and remediation as distinct decision tracks.
- Capture evidence and lessons learned early so the after-action review is usable.
