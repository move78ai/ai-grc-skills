# Third-Party AI / Supplier Risk

## What this skill does
Workflow-first supplier-risk guidance for due diligence, evidence requests, contract controls, monitoring, and fallback planning.

## Who it is for
- Teams assessing vendors, models, APIs, datasets, or embedded AI services.
- Operators requesting evidence and turning gaps into contract or approval conditions.
- Reviewers setting monitoring, fallback, and exit expectations for third-party AI.

## Use this skill when
- You need supplier due diligence or approval support.
- You need AI-specific contract, SLA, or evidence-request language.
- You need monitoring, contingency, or exit planning for a third-party AI dependency.

## Typical inputs
- Supplier scope, deployment model, data access, and business criticality.
- Existing contracts, security reviews, governance artifacts, and evidence already received.
- Fallback options, operational dependencies, and known risk signals.

## Typical outputs
- Due diligence pack, evidence request, or approval memo.
- Contract annex, monitoring plan, or contingency plan.
- Restricted approval, hold, or do-not-approve decision record.

## How it works
- Separate intake, evidence sufficiency, contract terms, and monitoring design.
- Convert unanswered supplier questions into explicit approval conditions.
- Keep fallback, decommission, and incident coordination visible from the start.
